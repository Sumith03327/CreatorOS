**Target: 2:45.** Rules require *screen recording + you on webcam*.
Webcam on for the open and close; screen for everything in between.

**Before you hit record**
- Dev server running, `/agents` already loaded (first compile is slow — don't film it).
- Gmail shows **Connected** in the Connections panel. *(Verified ACTIVE.)*

- Close other tabs. Zoom the browser to ~110% so text is readable at 1080p.

## 0:00 – 0:20 · Cold open (webcam)

> "Every AI tool for creators gives you a chat box. You ask it for a title, it invents something generic, and you have no idea if it's true.
>
> CreatorOS is different. These agents read expert playbooks, ground themselves in data you've actually proven works, and then **act on your real accounts**. Let me show you."

**Cut to screen. Do not explain the UI — just start using it.**

---

## 0:20 – 1:00 · Beat 1 — Thumbnails with your real face

*The unique thing. Lead with it.*

1. Open **Thumbnail Studio**.
2. Paste a channel URL. **Say out loud: "No photo. Just a link."**
3. It reads the channel's real thumbnails with vision — style *and* the recurring creator.
4. Generate. Show the result.

> "It read the channel's actual thumbnails, found the creator's face, and put **them** in a new one. I uploaded nothing.
>
> This didn't work at first — GPT-image-1 invented a stranger every time. We route the identity edits through Gemini's image model instead, and feed it the channel's own thumbnails as reference. Same gateway, different model, right tool for the job."

*(This is your Multi-model track story. Say it here, once, naturally.)*

---

## 1:00 – 1:40 · Beat 2 — An agent that reads your real inbox

*Proof it acts on real work.*

1. Open **Sponsorship Manager**. Click **Triage my deals**.
2. **Point at the Activity Rail as it runs** — this is the money shot:
   - `📚 Loading skill: Sponsorship & Rate Negotiation`
   - `🔌 Acting via Gmail…`
3. Real deals appear as cards.

> "That's my actual Gmail. It found the brand emails, and look —" *(point at the red ✗ marks)* "— it's flagging the terms the brand **didn't** specify. Usage rights. Exclusivity. Payment terms.
>
> It knows to check those because it loaded a negotiation playbook first. That's not in its prompt — it fetched it, on demand, because the task needed it."

4. Show the **rate calculator**: median views × CPM.

> "And it prices on views, never subscribers — straight out of the same playbook."

---

## 1:40 – 2:20 · Beat 3 — The agent that can't lie to you

*The strongest technical story. Slow down here.*

1. Open **Trend Scout**. Show the **Winning Formula** panel.
2. Click **Add data → Find winners**. Type a niche. Show real outliers appear with multiples (`40.2×`).

> "These are real videos that massively beat their own channel's average. I pick the ones I want, and now my agent grounds on *proven* data — not vibes."

3. Run the scan. Show the Opportunity Board with **evidence rows** attached to ideas.

> "Every idea cites the real video behind it.
>
> Here's the part I'm proudest of. When we built this, the model **fabricated** an outlier — a channel that didn't exist, with view counts it invented. Telling it 'don't hallucinate' didn't work.
>
> So we stopped asking. **The server checks every citation against what the agent actually saw**, and strips anything it can't prove. An agent here literally cannot cite a video it never found."
